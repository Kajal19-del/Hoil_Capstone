"Have a Happy Holi!"

HOLI - "Festival of Colors" or the "Festival of Love"

Holi has religious and mythological significance, marking the victory of good over evil and the arrival of spring. Holi is now celebrated globally by people of Indian origin and has become a symbol of India's cultural richness and diversity.

Main Program

Flower

Holi is celebrated on the full moon day in the Hindu month of Phalguna, which falls in February or March.

Love

Holi is also associated with several legends, such as the Love story of Lord Krishna and Radha.

Color

People throw colored powder and water on each other, sing and dance to traditional music, and feast on sweet delicacies.

Message

Holi is considered one of the major festivals in Hinduism and is celebrated with great fervor and enthusiasm.

Worship

The festival has a cultural significance, bringing people together and fostering unity and happiness.
